We are interested in various aspects of human genetics and genomics with a focus on the development of computational methods for the detection and analysis of genetic variation in the context of human disease. High-throughput sequencing technologies have revolutionized the study of human genetic variation and the discovery of genes and variants associated with risk for both rare and complex human diseases. Our goal is to to develop innovative ways for extracting useful information about human disease from large-scale genomic datasets. We have previously developed accurate computational methods for the detection of DNA sequence variants from high-throughput sequence data, statistical methods for association analysis of rare variants and cost-efficient approaches to enable targeted genome sequencing for thousands of individuals. Our research utilizes a combination of algorithms, probabilistic modeling, large-scale data analysis and population genetics. Recent and current research projects include: 
  • Computational methods for the detection of short indels from high-throughput sequence data 
  • Methods for  haplotype assembly or haplotypinghuman genomes using high-throughput sequencing technologies (computational methods and technology development)
  • DNA pooling based targeted sequencing of thousands of individuals
  • Predicting the functional impact of variants in coding regions of the human genome (focus on frameshift indels and variants affecting RNA splicing)
